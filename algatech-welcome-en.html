About Centre Algatech

Algatech – Centre of Algal Biotechnology is the largest microalgae research centre in the Czech Republic. We're based in the beautifully restored Opatovický Mill in Třeboň, as a detached facility of the Institute of Microbiology of the Czech Academy of Sciences. We bridge fundamental algal biology and applied biotechnology — and we genuinely enjoy it.

🔬Our five laboratories

  • Laboratory of Photosynthesis
  • Laboratory of Algal Cell Cycles
  • Laboratory of Anoxygenic Phototrophs
  • Laboratory of Algal Biotechnology
  • Application Laboratory of Algal Technologies

🏠Where to find us

We're located in the historic 18th-century Opatovický Mill, just below the Opatovický pond's dam, a short walk from the centre of Třeboň. The complex was extensively reconstructed in 2009–2014 and today houses laboratories and a fermentation hall.

Your first steps with us

No stress, no rigid day-by-day schedule — onboarding here is a friendly process that we walk through together with your supervisor and HR. Here are the main themes we cover along the way.

✉️

Before you arrive

We'll agree on your start date, accommodation and who will greet you on day one. Please send us copies of your ID/passport, diploma and CV in advance — it saves the running around on the first day.

👋

Your arrival and first days

We'll sign the contract together, give you a building tour and introduce you to your supervisor and a lab buddy. You'll get your access card, email, network credentials and key contacts.

🧷

What we'll handle together

  • We register you with the District Social Security Administration (OSSZ) — the employer must report your start within 8 calendar days (Sec. 94, Act 187/2006 Coll. on Sickness Insurance).
  • We notify your public health insurance fund (within 8 days, Sec. 10, Act 48/1997 Coll.). Just tell us which fund you're insured with.
  • We sign the income tax declaration with you so you can claim the basic taxpayer credit and any child credit (Sec. 38k, Act 586/1992 Coll. on Income Taxes).
  • We set up the attendance system and walk you through using it.
  • We show you the canteen, library, shared spaces and facilities.
  • We hand over the key info on payroll, leave (6 weeks/year) and benefits.
🛂

Residence formalities (if you're coming from abroad)

HR will guide you through any residence-related steps — registration with the Foreign Police, OAMP appointments at the Ministry of Interior and follow-up paperwork. We coordinate the timing together so you don't have to figure it out alone, and we'll go with you when it helps.

🧪

Lab induction

Before you start lab work, you'll do the mandatory occupational-health-and-safety and fire-safety training. Your supervisor and lab buddy will gradually walk you through equipment, chemicals and notebook conventions — no instrument is used without local training.

🏘️

Settling into Třeboň

Among the first steps are registering with a GP, opening a bank account and getting to know the town. The "Practical info" and "Life in Třeboň" sections have tips and contacts.

Important: there are no silly questions. If any step doesn't make sense, just write it in the HR form below — Markéta (Czech employees) or Jana (international colleagues). We'd rather double-check than have anything catch you off guard.

Salary, attendance & leave

A short orientation in how the working day, payroll and leave work here.

💶Salary and payslip

Net salary = gross − social security − health insurance − sickness insurance − income tax + applied tax reliefs. Salary lands on your Czech bank account by the 10th of the following month. You'll receive a monthly payslip.

🕘Working hours

Full-time is 40 hours per week. We work with flexible hours — the daily core is agreed with your supervisor. Lab work naturally follows experiments, so stay in close touch with your team.

🗓️Attendance

Each person records their own attendance in the electronic system. Absences — leave, sickness, home office, business trips, conferences — are agreed with your supervisor and marked in the system. HR closes the monthly record.

🌴Annual leave

Our employees receive 6 weeks of paid annual leave per year (pro-rated for part-time or partial year). Plan it with your supervisor in advance — especially around key experiments and conferences.

🤒Sick leave & doctor visits

If you can't come in, let your supervisor and HR know as soon as possible. For longer illness, your GP issues an electronic sick note that is sent automatically to us and to the social-security office.

🎁Benefits

Meal contribution (paid through payroll), performance bonuses, conference travel support, language courses on request, and access to the Czech Academy of Sciences benefits programme.

Personal income tax (2026)

A short overview of the most common questions. Specific cases — multiple employers, additional contracts, deductions — are always handled by payroll (Markéta) or a tax advisor.

📊2026 rates and basic relief

Two-rate progressive tax: 15% up to the legal threshold (about CZK 1.76 million/year) and 23% above. Standard taxpayer relief for 2026 is CZK 30,840 per year.

🧮Employee mandatory contributions

From gross salary: 7.1% social + 4.5% health + 0.6% sickness insurance. The employer pays its share on top.

📑Annual settlement vs. own return

If you have only one employer for the whole year, payroll usually performs the annual settlement. With multiple employers, additional income or non-standard reliefs, you file your own return — typically by 1 April (with extensions for electronic filing or via a tax advisor).

👨‍👩‍👧Family reliefs and common deductions

Child credits in 2026: ~CZK 15,204 (1st), 22,320 (2nd), 27,840 (3rd+). Spouse relief CZK 24,840 if spouse earns under CZK 68,000/year and cares for a child under 3. Plus deductions for donations, supplementary pension, life insurance and selected housing-loan interest.

🌍Tax residence (for international colleagues)

If your permanent home or centre of vital interests is in the Czech Republic, or you spend at least 183 days here in a calendar year, you're typically a Czech tax resident. Otherwise — non-resident, taxed only on Czech-source income. Double-tax treaties may apply to your home country.

🪪Certificate of tax domicile

If you need to prove Czech tax residence to a foreign tax authority, you can apply for a Certificate of Tax Domicile at your local tax office. HR will issue the employment confirmation you need to attach.

Rates, thresholds and deadlines change every year. Markéta always uses the current valid version — if you're unsure, just ask.

Residence & visa — we go through it together

If you're moving to the Czech Republic, the residence side of things looks more complicated than it really is — because we walk through it with you. HR coordinates the visits to the Foreign Police and the OAMP office (Ministry of Interior), helps with appointments and is happy to come along when it's useful.

🇪🇺EU / EFTA citizens

No visa or work permit is needed. For longer stays, a Certificate of Temporary Residence is recommended — it makes banking, healthcare and family registrations much smoother. We help you prepare the application and book the OAMP appointment.

🔬Researchers from third countries

The standard pathway is the long-term residence permit for the purpose of scientific research, based on a Hosting Agreement we issue. The permit is valid up to 2 years and gives free access to the Czech labour market — no separate work permit. HR prepares the hosting agreement together with you.

🪪Non-research staff from third countries

For technical, administrative and other non-research roles, the typical permit is the Employee Card (zaměstnanecká karta) — a combined work-and-residence permit. HR coordinates the steps with you.

📍Registering your address (HR coordinates)

Once you arrive, your place of residence needs to be registered. We don't leave you to handle this alone — HR coordinates the Foreign Police and OAMP visits, helps with the timing and gets the right paperwork ready.

📷Biometrics & residence card

After arrival you'll book a Ministry of Interior appointment for biometrics (fingerprints and a photo). The biometric residence card is then ready to collect within roughly 30–60 days. HR helps you set this up and reminds you of follow-up steps.

👨‍👩‍👧Family members

A spouse, registered partner and minor children can apply for residence on family-reunion grounds, linked to your status. We can help you prepare the document checklist (marriage and birth certificates with apostille or superlegalisation, certified Czech translations).

The short version: just keep us in the loop on your travel dates. HR handles the legwork on appointments, paperwork and timing — you focus on settling in.

Lab culture

A few simple habits that help you settle in quickly and work safely.

👋Say hi, ask anything

Greet colleagues when entering rooms or offices. We work in two languages — feel free to ask people to slow down or switch to English.

🛠️Train before you touch

No instrument is used without local training and supervisor approval — even if you've used a similar one elsewhere. SOPs (Standard Operating Procedures — written step-by-step protocols posted in the lab) and the lab notebook are not optional.

🤝Shared resources

Use the equipment and consumables of your own group. If you need something from another lab, ask the responsible person first. Where there's a booking system, use it.

🚨Report issues immediately

Freezer alarms, water leaks, broken glass, unusual smells — report to your lab manager and maintenance straight away. Faster reporting = less damage.

🧼Tidy and well — part of safety

Personal hygiene and a tidy bench are part of BOZP (occupational health & safety): wipe up spills you create, don't leave wet floors behind you, and please stay home if you feel unwell — protecting colleagues and shared equipment matters.

📚Borrow responsibly

Return borrowed items on time. If you need to keep them longer, leave a clear note. Same applies to literature and shared drives.

💬We're direct — please be direct too

Czech communication tends to be straightforward. It's perfectly normal — and welcome — to ask about money directly (e.g. "you covered my lunch, how much do I owe you?"). What can feel impolite in some cultures is the everyday way here. The same goes for borrowed items, deadlines or favours: a clear, friendly reminder is appreciated, not rude.

🗣️It's OK to push back

The hierarchy is clear, but talking openly with your supervisor is expected and welcome. If you're already running an experiment and can't realistically start another one in parallel, say so — even (especially) to your boss. "I have other work right now, I'll get to it on Thursday" is a perfectly fine answer. Don't agree to something you can't deliver; that creates more friction than honesty does.

🎯Aim for independence

Take notes, ask questions and write your own SOPs. The goal is for you to feel confident and safe working independently as soon as possible.

Life in Třeboň

Třeboň is a small historic town in South Bohemia, surrounded by ponds, forests and a UNESCO biosphere reserve. A calm pace, nature on the doorstep and a strong cultural tradition.

🚴Nature and sport

Cycle paths and walking trails around the famous fishponds (Svět, Rožmberk), through pine forests and peat bogs. Swimming, running, paddling and ice-skating are favourites among the team.

🏰Culture and history

Renaissance castle, historic square, brewery and the Schwarzenberg tomb are within walking distance. Třeboň is famous for autumn pond harvests, summer concerts and the film festival.

♨️Spa and wellness

Třeboň is a traditional peat-spa town. Třeboň Spa has pools, saunas and procedures open to residents too — perfect for unwinding. There's also Fitness na Kraji Světa and Fitnes Třeboň for regular workouts.

👨‍👩‍👧Family and schools

There are nurseries, kindergartens, primary and secondary schools. Nursery capacity is limited — apply early. International schools are available in České Budějovice and Prague.

🗺️Day trips nearby

České Budějovice, Český Krumlov, Hluboká Castle, the Šumava mountains and the Austrian border are an easy drive away. Vienna and Prague are a few hours by car.

🍽️Where to eat

Favourite spots among colleagues, all in Třeboň:

  • Curry House — Indian
  • Oregano — Italian
  • Sahara
  • Krčma u Kellyho
  • Bistro 105

Plus the local Regent brewery and cafés on the main square.

🎬Culture in town

In Třeboň you'll find Cinema Třeboň, Galerie 105 and seasonal events on the main square. Local traditions include the autumn pond harvests, summer concerts and the film festival.

🗣️Czech language

English is widely used at the centre, but a bit of Czech makes daily life much easier. For international colleagues, we're happy to arrange a language course — just write to Jana.
